    • Now that we have your attention, let us introduce you to The Tackle Box - our meticulously curated toolkit designed to empower you in the pursuit of combating inequality. If you are looking for ways to combat inequality then you have come to the right place. On this page, you will find resources and links to accredited researchers in the field who have devoted their lives to working on inequality in women in the workplace.

    Additionally, you’ll uncover straightforward actions that can spark transformative change in both your professional and personal life - trust us, the Mrs. will thank you.

    There’s another issue that cropped up a fair few times for us during the curation of this website and that’s when men interview women for jobs. Find out all the details below, it’s okay - we’re here to help.

    Whether you’re out on the boat or stuck in the office, this guide will only enhance your life. Do us a favor and help to hook, line and sink inequality.

    Ever had your Mrs come home and complain about some jerk from work?

    Look, this is really the easiest thing you can do - don’t be a tool. Have you ever considered that you might be the jerk at your office? Because odds aren’t good if you’ve been given our details by a colleague.


    But alas, we are here to help! It’s likely that you’re currently thinking “I’m not a tool… Am I?” Let’s make this simple - if you follow these guidelines the likelihood that you remain a tool is slim.


    Change begins with acceptance so please, accept it and move forward. Let’s begin

    Photo: By dusanpetkovic1, Adobe Stock, Remixed by Kate Wise

    There are a few common themes when it comes to inequality, and this one is BIG. Women aren’t being heard. In a meeting with ten people, its takes eight of them to be female before their voices are heard as much as the mens.


    Whether that’s in a meeting or serving customers. There seems to be a common denominator in all of this: men don’t realise how much their voices dominate a conversation. So, here’s our tip: sit back, relax, and ask for your female colleague’s opinion. Her ideas may surpass Steve’s from accounting, he is unbelievably boring after all.


    Often, when a woman voices an idea, it isn’t heard until a man says it. So, here’s another tip - reiterate what she said to the whole group and give her credit. You would not believe how much more your office will retain hard-working femmes just by following this one single tip.

    There’s a time and place for shit yarns with the boys and we believe spending a day out on the boat talking shit and sinking piss is completely inevitable. What isn’t ok is doing it in the office, or even the lunchroom. We get it, there’s plenty of fish in the sea but the whole office doesn’t need to hear about it.


    So here’s our next tip - keep the sharns for Saturdays with the boys. We’re not cancelling the boys club; we’re just canceling it between the hours of 9-5 Monday to Friday - does that sound reasonable?

    Tips for interviewing women

    As a man, have you ever been asked whether or not you plan to have children in the next year? No? Well most of the women we interviewed have been. So please, it’s illegal not to hire someone based on their maternal requirements so let’s cut that line nice and quick.

    Is it ok to ask about their family then? This one is tricky and we suggest not to. If they want to tell a virtual stranger about their beautiful newborn then they will. However it can be seen as fishing on your behalf if you ask.

    Ok so we need another woman in the office to even out the men and do the event planning cause we suck at it. Well, did you state this in the interview? Don’t reel them in with tempting careers only to snag them in the rocks event planning. Be transparent.

    Fish on! Great, you’ve hired a woman! ..Now how do you keep her on the line? Losing a jig is rough but losing someone due to ‘cultural differences’ is far worse. Make sure to keep reading to understand how you can retain more female staff in the future.
